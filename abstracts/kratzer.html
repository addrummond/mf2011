Predicting the space of possible pronouns

The talk will explore some consequences of my 2008 paper Making a Pronoun for predicting the range of possible bound variable pronouns in natural languages. The major claims are (a) that all bound pronouns are born alike, (b) that the binders for bound variable pronouns are not carried by antecedent DPs, but by functional heads like v or C, and that (c) bound variables acquire the characteristic looks of personal, reflexive, or relative pronouns, for example, from the functional heads that carry their binders.
