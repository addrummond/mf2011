Bound Individual Variables in Pronouns: Are there any?

Pronouns are claimed to be covert definite descriptions containing a locally free situation variable but no individual variables. Donkey anaphora and related phenomena are analysed very much as in my book Situations and Individuals (2005). C-commanded bound variable pronouns are now analysed in the same way, i.e. by means of situation variables being bound. Referential uses of pronouns involve these situation variables being used referentially; and descriptive indexical uses (as in Nunberg's work) involve descriptive content being contributed by NP-deletion (which does not, of course, always require a linguistic antecedent).
